Our Team

Dmitrii Belousov

Dmitrii is an experienced software engineer who moved to the United States back in 2019. Shortly after arriving, his family needed a $10,000 loan to pay for a university summer program for their daughter. He visited dozens of lending institutions, but none were willing to issue a loan because he did not have a domestic credit score. Dmitrii had years of credit history back in Russia - but it was completely ignored. He had to start from scratch. Eventually, Dmitrii managed to get a loan from an online lending company, subject to an upfront fee and a high interest rate.

A year later, Dmitrii finally accumulated a domestic credit score and got access to common credit products. As you can imagine, he endured a frustrating and stressful period -- but it inspired him to try to fix the problem.

Douglas Fisher

Douglas is a professional software leader, manager, and developer of many years experience. He’s held leadership roles in six Independent Software Vendors (ISVs), including two greenfield startups. Douglas shepherded one of those greenfield startups (barometerIT) to acquisition and subsequent growth. He also has diverse experience as a technical consultant for three consulting groups.

Douglas has been working in the Java (JVM) and JavaScript ecosystems since the earliest public releases of each. He’s been hands-on with very many of the techniques, patterns, and frameworks developed in both ecosystems over that period. Douglas received his BA from Saint Olaf College, Northfield, Minnesota. To learn more, see his own site.

Civis Mundi

The Latin words, "civis mundi", mean "citizen of the world". Given our goals, this choice of name seems appropriate. According to scholars of Latin, the word "civis" is pronounced with a hard "c" and a soft "v" (/ˈkiːʋis/).
